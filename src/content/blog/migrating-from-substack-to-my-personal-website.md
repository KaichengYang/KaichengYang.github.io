## TL;DR

I migrated my blog from Substack to my personal website for a more seamless integration into my AI agent workflow.

## Why

I started writing on Substack at https://yang3kc.substack.com around Nov 2023.
I chose the platform because I didn't want to manage my own blog system (formatting, hosting, RSS, etc.) and it had a lot of nice features.
It worked, and I was happy with it.

But if you check my list of posts, it's easy to tell that I'm not the most diligent writer---not because I don't have things to write about, but because of laziness (and life, of course).

Things have changed recently.
First, for the past few months, I have been experimenting with reorganizing my knowledge system around AI agents---Claude Code, to be specific.
The system still needs a lot of iterations, but it's clear that there is no turning back.
Second, with AI agents, it's very easy to maintain a blog system.

I'll write about my knowledge system later, but the key principle is that everything should be recorded in plain text and AI agents should have easy access to it.
Under this principle, any services and platforms with relatively closed ecosystems and no easy way for AI integration have no place in my knowledge system.
So it became obvious that I needed to migrate my blog from Substack to my personal website.

I'm still iterating on the new blog system, but it's quite straightforward.
All posts are written in markdown files and then rendered as HTML.
Any AI agent can easily handle this automatically.

## What's next

My goal is to become a more diligent writer.
I already take notes frequently, and I hope to turn them into more public-facing posts that can be shared.
As a disclaimer, AI will be heavily involved in my writing process because this is the only way I can realistically achieve my goal.
